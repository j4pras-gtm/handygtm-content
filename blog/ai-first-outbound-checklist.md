Most AI-outbound failures are upstream of the model. Bad ICP, thin enrichment, no signal layer, no feedback loop. Blasting AI-generated "personalization" at a cold, unverified list is just a faster way to burn your domain reputation.

Modern GTM Engineering isn't about using AI to write more emails; it's about using AI to ensure you are only reaching out when the timing is perfect. Run this 9-point checklist before you let an LLM near your outbound sequences.

## 1. Job Change Detection
Timing is everything. A past champion moving to a new company or a new executive hire at a target account is the strongest signal in B2B. Use tools like UserGems or Clay to track these movements. If you don't have a "New in Role" filter, your AI is guessing.

## 2. Funding & Growth Signals
A Series B announcement isn't just a "congrats" opportunity; it's a signal of budget and shifting priorities. Use AI to parse the funding announcement for specific keywords related to your product's value prop.

## 3. Technographic Spikes
Is a target account suddenly installing a competitor's tool? Or perhaps they just added a complementary piece of tech? Use technographic data (via BuiltWith or HG Insights) to trigger sequences that mention how you integrate or compare.

## 4. Intent Data Filtering
Don't reach out just because they fit the ICP. Use intent signals (G2, 6sense, or Bombora) to verify they are actually in a buying cycle. AI can help summarize *what* they are researching so your outreach is context-aware.

## 5. Persona-Pain Point Alignment
AI excels at mapping. Feed your LLM your ICP's common pain points and have it verify if the prospect's LinkedIn profile or recent posts actually mention those challenges. If there's no alignment, don't send.

## 6. Multi-channel Sequencing
Outbound isn't just email. Your checklist must include LinkedIn engagement and potentially cold calling. AI can draft the LinkedIn connection request and the call script based on the same core signal to ensure consistency.

## 7. AI Personalization Depth
Avoid the "I saw you went to [University]" trap. Your AI should be looking for "Deep Personalization"—referencing a specific quote from a podcast, a line from a recent 10-K filing, or a specific technology they use.

## 8. Response Sentiment Analysis
When the replies start coming in, don't treat them all the same. Use AI to categorize responses into "Interested," "Not Interested," or "Referral." This allows you to automate the next step (e.g., booking a meeting vs. removing from list).

## 9. Meeting Handoff Automation
The final check: what happens when they say yes? Ensure your system is wired to automatically assign the lead in the CRM and notify the AE. Speed to lead is still the ultimate conversion driver.

---

**The Golden Rule:** If you wouldn't send the email manually, don't let an AI send it automatically. Use this checklist to move from "spray and pray" to "signal and solve."
